Bio

I am a PhD candidate at Stanford University working with Professor Lihua Lei in the Graduate School of Business on sequential testing, multiple testing, and long-term effect inference. Our paper on FWER control for e-values is on arXiv! I am also working with my advisor and the experiments team at DoorDash to develop short-term proxies for long-term metrics based on historical data, which I am presenting at CODE@MIT 2025 and hope to have a draft available soon.

Prior to attending Stanford, I earned my A.B. in Mathematics and Statistics Cum Laude with High Honors, with a Secondary in Music, from Harvard University. For my degree, I wrote a review of compressed sensing for my thesis with my advisor Subhabrata Sen.

I have a strong passion for teaching, and I have been the primary instructor for two Stanford statistics courses, twice each. I am part of the curriculum redevelopment team for Stanford Statistics' Introduction to Probability sequence, working on sections and leading a pilot tutorial program. I was the coach for the first year statistics PhD students in their Summer 2024 applied statistics qualifying exam. Everyone passed!
