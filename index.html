Bio

I am a PhD candidate at Stanford University working with Professor Lihua Lei in the Graduate School of Business on sequential testing and multiple testing. We have a rough draft of our paper on FWER control for e-values available here. I also am interested in random graph models and am discussing properties of the geometric random graph model with Professor Tselil Schramm of Stanford Statistics.

Prior to attending Stanford, I earned my A.B. in Mathematics and Statistics Cum Laude with High Honors, with a Secondary in Music, from Harvard University. For my degree, I wrote a review of compressed sensing for my thesis with my advisor Subhabrata Sen.

I have a strong passion for teaching, and I have been the primary instructor for two Stanford statistics courses. I will be reprising my role as instructor of Stats 217: Stochastic Processes I this summer quarter, and I am excited to be coaching the first year statistics PhD students in their applied statistics qualifying exam.

Talks and Posters

  • CODE@MIT, October 2023: Multiple A/B Testing with Always-Valid e-values. Joint work with Lihua Lei; presented algorithms to compute the graphical approach for FWER control with (always-valid) e-values using a weighted local test. Rough draft here
  • Experimentation and Causal Inference, Stanford, June 2023: Multiple A/B Testing with Always-Valid e-values.
  • Stanford Statistics Retreat, May 2023: Once Upon a Stream: Mining for Significance. Presented on a real-world example of multiple testing and selective inference analysis done in the context of testing for cheating in a 2020 Minecraft speedrun.
